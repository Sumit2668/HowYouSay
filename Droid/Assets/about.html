Why We Made "How You Say"

In the summer of 2015, my wife, Angie, our eldest daughter Maddie, and I headed to Europe. For the first part we volunteered at a kid’s camp in Gjakova, Kosovo with Service International. After that, Angie and I celebrated our 20th year of marriage in Sorrento and Rome, Italy.

I love languages, and what it teaches us about the way people think and communicate. I couldn’t find any really good apps or resources for Albanian, the dominant language in Kosovo, so I built one. I needed something to quickly capture vocabulary and document how to say it.

Because I couldn’t go to a reference book, or website, the people I met became my source. I hadn’t anticipated just how rich and rewarding that would be. It completely changed how I view language learning. Over the course of 3 weeks, I captured dozens of words and phrases, both in text and audio, and rapidly modified the app to be as useful as possible. And oh how useful it was.

From this trip came “How You Say”. The emphasis is on learning language (and culture) through engaging local speakers. Real people. It doesn’t give you a computer translation. You don’t get a computer pronounciation. You get the people you record, and each recording takes you right back to that person, that conversion, and that setting.

How You Say promotes curiosity and encourages you to be inquisitive in order to build a working vocabulary. It is your personal language journal.

Give it an honest try. I think you’ll really love it.

- Dave Ortinau, Co-Founder of Rendr
